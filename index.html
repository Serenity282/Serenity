Literature Survey

The WHO states that 1 in 8 individuals (around 970 million people) has a diagnosed mental disorder [1]

Figure 1. Annual coconut production 2011 - 2021 [1]

The field of mental health and A.I. research, particularly stress level prediction, is currently active.[3] Studies have explored links between stress and physiological factors like heart rate variability, cortisol levels, and skin conductance. For instance, one study [4] used heartbeats to predict stress. Wearable devices like smartwatches provide real-time physiological data and have been used in stress prediction. In a different research paper [5], a model used data from a wearable Photoplethysmography sensor for stress prediction. Various research papers have addressed stress management questionnaires. Hemphill Pearson and Barbara J. developed an eight-session stress management questionnaire, while another study [3] introduced the DASS42 scale for identifying depression, anxiety, and stress via a questionnaire. Machine learning algorithms, specifically Support Vector Machine and Logistic Regression, achieved high accuracy in predicting these conditions, around 97% and 98%, respectively. Prior research [4] delved into deep learning techniques for detecting the mental states of users. Collecting and analyzing user data is crucial for improving the accuracy of machine learning and deep learning algorithms in detecting stress levels effectively.

Self-reflection and journaling practices have gained recognition for their role in enhancing self-awareness and emotional regulation. The study by Smith et al. [6] (2019) emphasized the benefits of regular journaling in fostering personal growth. Effective features facilitating self-reflection, as highlighted in the research, include free writing, guided prompts, and structured exercises, offering flexibility and guidance. In addition to tracking emotional well-being, the incorporation of activity suggestion features provides individuals with practical recommendations for maintaining and enhancing their mental health. These suggestions may include activities like engaging in physical exercise, practicing mindfulness, connecting with a supportive community, or pursuing creative hobbies. Natural language processing (NLP) systems, like BioBERT and ClinicalBERT, play a crucial role in understanding journal content and emotional tone, classifying entries into positive, negative, or neutral categories (Garcia et al., 2021).[7] The research of Johnson et al. (2017) introduced a sentiment analysis system for tracking emotional state changes through journal entry analysis, effectively illustrating mood fluctuations. Combining sentiment analysis with data visualizations empowers individuals to monitor mental well-being and personal growth over time[8].

Positive thinking, feelings, and emotions are acknowledged are recognized for their role in improving mental health [11]. DeMuse is a mood-based music application that emerged from a study exploring music's influence on stress reduction [15]. Additionally, nature sounds are proven effective for physiological recovery after stress [13]. The Stress Reduction Theory by Ulrich and the Attention Restoration Theory by Kaplan [10] both highlight nature's positive impact on emotions and focus. Nature's strong link to mental balance aids stress coping, particularly evident during the COVID-19 pandemic [9]. This connection leads to therapies like guided imagery, mindfulness, and eco-therapy for mental health [9]. Guided imagery therapy's effectiveness is statistically supported [17]. During the COVID lockdown, nature videos reduce stress and negativity, irrespective of age or video duration [15]. Similarly, research suggests that dog videos reduce anxiety and increase positivity more than nature videos [12]. Facial Emotion Recognition (FER) is explored in therapy [14]. A CNN-based approach achieved 80% accuracy for four emotions [14]. According to the authors of [16], online therapy is not a novel concept because it has been in practice even before 2016. Emotion recognition systems such as Emotient and Affectiva are used in marketing and media analytics. Existing mobile applications like Calm and Headspace aid mindfulness, meditation, and relaxation, supported by research. An R.C.T. affirms the effectiveness of the Calm app in establishing mindfulness and favorable mental outcomes [29]. Woebot is an AI chatbot delivering cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) for anxiety and depression symptoms, offering personalized suggestions.

In the realm of text-based image generation, several remarkable systems and tools have emerged, each offering its distinctive features and capabilities. Notably, DALL·E, an innovation from OpenAI, stands out as a pioneering model in this field. DALL·E. [19] seamlessly combines GAN architecture with Transformer models to create images based on textual descriptions. For instance, it can take a text prompt like "a Male Lion walking in the jungle" and skillfully produce images that vividly bring this description to life Another noteworthy system, AttnGAN, distinguishes itself by harnessing an attention mechanism to generate highly detailed and coherent images from textual descriptions. AttnGAN [20] adeptly combines global and local attention, refining the generated images for visually stunning results. This system excels in transforming textual descriptions into images replete with fine-grained details, making it particularly well-suited for creative applications like art generation.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Stress Prediction

Researchers in the field have primarily focused on machine learning and IoT-based approaches to predict stress levels. However, a research gap exists regarding the most relevant predictive factors and their interactions. Many existing studies require costly and complex hardware, limiting accessibility. Moreover, they often lack comprehensive and multimodal feature incorporation. The proposed system streamlines data collection with the involvement of mental health experts and prioritizes user-centered design.

Approaches for Stress Reduction

Research reveals significant gaps in understanding the effectiveness of complementary health approaches, mindfulness interventions, and yoga in improving mental well-being, particularly in managing stress. Motivations, outcomes, and the efficacy of these interventions are inadequately addressed. Additionally, there's a need for meta-analyses in assessing the effects of Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) and Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT) on mental health.

Mindfulness and Mental Health Apps

The research identifies a gap in the accurate assessment of a user's mental health condition within existing mental health apps. To address this gap, it suggests leveraging methods like voice or emotion recognition, which could provide more precise insights into a user's emotional state. Additionally, the integration of facial recognition and AI technologies could offer a means to tailor therapy recommendations more personally, based on the individual's emotional and mental condition.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to mitigate challenges in addressing mental health disorders and improve mental well being with the utilization of advanced technologies ?

The COVID-19 pandemic has led to a significant increase in mental health issues due to factors like isolation, monotony, heavy workloads, and financial stress. Beyond the pandemic, the competitive and high-pressure modern world poses ongoing challenges to mental well-being. Many people lack access to advanced mental health solutions, and addressing mental health is inherently complex. Efforts to digitize mental health approaches are underway, but ensuring the effectiveness of these technology-driven methods remains a key challenge. This underscores the need for innovative approaches to bridge the gap between traditional and digital mental health solutions in an ever-evolving world.


Proposed Solution

Coco Remedy uses mobile and web-based software to manage Weligama Coconut Leaf Wilt Disease (WCLWD) and Coconut Caterpillar Infestation (CCI), which devastate coconut cultivation. In WCLWD, yellowing of leaves which also associates with Magnesium deficiency makes it difficult to distinguish the diseased palms accurately. Similarly, the dried appearance of leaves due to CCI is difficult to distinguish from leaf scorching. Therefore, Deep Learning techniques like Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) for feature extraction will be used to identify the diseases accurately. Coconut growers, Estate managers of plantation companies, Researches of Coconut Research Institute (CRISL), Coconut Development Officers (CDOs) of Coconut Cultivation Board (CCB), and the general public will be able to identify diseased palm accurately by capturing a photograph of leaf with symptoms. The location will be extracted by Geo Tags in order to gather anonymous data using OpenWeatherMapAPI for future predictions. The severity of WCLWD is calculated based on the multiple symptoms using CNN. Since caterpillars’ associates with the damaged leaves in CCI, counts of caterpillars are taken using OpenCV / YOLO object detection while the infected area is calculated using MASK R-CNN. Using the above-mentioned parameters progression level is determined. Real-time notifications are sent whenever an infected tree is found. Details of the infected trees, location and severity records enable the researches and CDOs to take remedial actions. Infected areas and danger zones which might be affected in the future can visualized through Google mapping technologies. Nearby users are notified to take precautions before their lands get affected.

Research Objectives

Stress Prediction Through Deep Learning

The first objective is to create a deep learning model capable of accurately predicting an individual's stress levels using a range of factors, including environmental, behavioral, and biological variables. This model is intended to aid individuals and healthcare providers in the early identification and management of stress, thus preventing it from progressing into a chronic condition.

AI based self reflective journaling and activity suggestion

The second objective of this project is to develop an AI-powered self-reflection and journaling system that serves as a valuable tool for users to understand their thoughts and emotions. Furthermore, assists users in identifying and implementing effective strategies to manage their mental health effectively.

Facial Emotion Recognition & Video Recommendation

The third objective is to enhance the understanding of users by employing machine learning techniques for emotion recognition. This involves analyzing and identifying the emotional states of users as they interact with the application. Once the user's emotions are successfully recognized, the subsequent step is to provide personalized therapeutic videos that are specifically tailored to match the user's identified emotional state.

Generate custom images from user text input.

The fourth objective is to Offering users the ability to create calming images can be a powerful tool for stress reduction. This feature allows individuals to engage in a therapeutic and creative process, providing a welcome respite from the demands of everyday life. By generating soothing visuals, users can find relaxation and solace in the act of artistic expression. This not only serves as a means of self-care but also fosters a sense of accomplishment and well-being.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Stress Prediction Through Deep Learning.
  2. AI based self reflective journaling and activity suggestion.
  3. Facial Emotion Recognition & Video Recommendation.
  4. Generate custom images from user text input.

Fig 4 illustrates the overall system diagram of the proposed solution which was intended to provide a smart approach for stakeholders, researchers, and Coconut Development Officers (CDOs) to detect the coconut diseases and pest infestations that may affect the coconut industry. As shown in the diagram, the registered users of the system can capture or upload the images which are suspicious. The images are sent to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) backend server where the flask server is deployed. These images are processed in the flask server by the designed DCNN models for disease identification. If WCLWD is found, the symptom severity will be determined using CNN models. Simultaneously, if CCI is identified, the images will go through the Mask-R-CNN model to determine the progression level while the number of caterpillars are extracted using the YOLOv5 object detection algorithm. Images will be classified using the CNN models of Mg deficiency and LSD at the same time.

Once the system identifies that the leaves are infected, then the response will be captured by the crowdsourcing platform. The Google Map will be updated with the real time locations (latitude and longitude) of the infected palms. In Addition, the system will automatically send notifications to the farmers and other stakeholders who are at the risk of infection.
